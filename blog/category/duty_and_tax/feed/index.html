Let’s dive in!

Pricing to Add Carrier Rates on Shopify Plan

On the basic Shopify plan, carrier-calculated rates from third parties like UPS are not enabled by default. However, you can add this capability for an additional $20 per month by upgrading your subscription.

The Advanced and Plus Shopify plans include activating carrier rates for free without any extra fees needed. So upgrading to one of these upper tiers is another route to enabling the feature.

If selling internationally to markets like Canada is a core part of your ecommerce strategy, the slightly higher cost of an Advanced plan can pay for itself quickly through the benefits of calculated shipping.

How to Activate Carrier Rates on Shopify

Once you’ve added carrier calculated shipping to your Shopify subscription, it’s straightforward to toggle it on:

  1. Navigate to Settings > Shipping and Delivery in your Shopify admin.
  2. Under “Carrier shipping”, click on “Enable” or “Upgrade your plan” if needed.
  3. Select the shipping zone you want to add rates for. Often this will be a “Rest of World” zone for international orders.
  4. Click “Add rate” and choose “Use carrier or app to calculate rates” when prompted.
  5. Search for and select the carrier accounts you wish to use, like UPS, FedEx, or USPS.
  6. Connect your carrier accounts by entering your account credentials when prompted.

That’s it! Shopify will now pull in live shipping rates from those carriers at checkout anytime an international customer views shipping options to their destination. No more having to manually update static shipping prices.

Turning On Carrier Rates for New Stores

If you want to activate carrier calculated shipping for additional new Shopify stores later on, you’ll need to follow a slightly different process. It’s worth noting that this applies not only to new stores but to all stores below the Advanced Plan threshold.

Rather than enabling it directly from your store’s shipping settings, which notably lacks the toggle for the $20 option, you will have to get in touch with Shopify support. This can be done either through chat or email. Inform them that you wish to enable carrier rates for your store subscription, and they will assist in activating the feature swiftly.

This extra step is due to how carrier rate permissions function across store accounts.

Reaching out to Shopify support via a chat is often the quickest way to get carrier rates switched on for a new store. Usually you’ll be connected with an advisor within minutes who can enable it while you wait.

Getting Support When You Need It

Speaking of Shopify support, chatting with advisors is the best way to get assistance if you run into any hiccups setting up calculated shipping.

Here are some key benefits of using Shopify’s chat-based customer support:

  • Instant 1-on-1 connection with knowledgeable shipping experts
  • Ability to share screenshots to tackle configuration issues
  • Guidance specific to your store’s setup and subscription tier
  • No need to refresh email repeatedly awaiting replies

Of course, you can also reach Shopify support via email or phone if preferred. But chat is often the fastest, most effective option for solving time-sensitive issues.

Basics of Canadian Customs and Duties

Customs duties are essentially taxes levied on goods when they cross international borders. In the context of e-commerce, these duties can significantly impact the supply chain, affecting everything from product pricing to delivery times. The difference between duties and taxes is subtle but crucial: while duties are based on the product type and origin, taxes are typically based on the product’s sale price.

For e-commerce businesses in Canada, understanding these nuances is essential. The Trade Commissioner’s guide provides an in-depth look into the world of online commerce, emphasizing the importance of customs duties.

Navigating the Customs Process

E-commerce businesses must be meticulous when dealing with customs. Accurate documentation, as highlighted by Canada Post, is the first step towards a smooth customs process. This ensures that products are correctly classified and duties are accurately calculated.

Inspections are another critical aspect. While the primary inspection verifies documentation, the secondary inspection delves deeper, examining the goods themselves. Being prepared for both can save businesses time and money.

Understanding Duties and Taxes in Depth

The Canadian De Minimis Threshold is the value below which goods can be shipped duty-free. This threshold varies based on trade agreements and can be a game-changer for e-commerce businesses. For instance, goods from countries within certain free trade zones might enjoy more lenient thresholds, as detailed in the IMF’s guide.

Who pays these duties and taxes? Typically, the importer of record is responsible. However, e-commerce businesses can sometimes shift this responsibility to consumers or use platforms like BorderBuddy to streamline the process.

Shipping Considerations for E-commerce Businesses

Shipping to and from Canada comes with its own set of rules. From product restrictions to prohibited imports, businesses must be aware of all regulations. Especially when shipping to places like the U.S., understanding nuances like food shipping or electronic goods regulations is essential. The USITC guide provides valuable insights into these aspects.

Role of Technology in Streamlining Customs and Duties

Technology has revolutionized the way e-commerce businesses handle customs duties. Platforms like Shopify have integrated tools that simplify duty calculations, ensuring businesses remain compliant while optimizing their supply chain. The Baranov CPA guide delves deeper into how technology is shaping e-commerce taxation.

Case Study: Successful E-commerce Businesses Navigating Canadian Customs

Real-life examples provide invaluable insights. Take, for instance, a business leveraging the Carnet program to ship samples duty-free. Such strategies can significantly reduce costs and improve customer satisfaction.

The local market in Canada is ripe for e-commerce growth. With the Government of Canada actively promoting digital platforms and online business, there’s never been a better time for businesses to expand their reach. However, understanding customs duty relief, exemptions, and import controls is essential for seamless cross-border transactions.

B2B E-commerce in Canada

While much of the focus is on consumer e-commerce, B2B (business-to-business) e-commerce sales are also on the rise. Canadian companies are increasingly leveraging online platforms to source goods and services, making it essential for exporters to understand the nuances of sales tax, VAT, and other duties and taxes due.

E-commerce Sales and the Canadian Dollar

The strength of the Canadian dollar plays a pivotal role in e-commerce sales. A strong currency can make imports cheaper, while a weaker one can boost exports. Understanding currency fluctuations and their impact on duties and taxes is crucial for e-commerce businesses.

Government of Canada’s Role in E-commerce

  • Canadian Customs Tariff: This is a comprehensive list that classifies and provides duty rates for all goods.
